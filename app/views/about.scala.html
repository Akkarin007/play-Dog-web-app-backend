What is Dog?

Dog is a board game for 2 to 6 players. The game is like the classic German board game "Mensch Ã¤rgere dich nicht". In order to win you must bring your 4 game pieces to safety. The main difference is that the pieces are not moved with dices. They are moved with the help of cards.

Gameplay:

The game starts with the first player playing a card. Its effect is then executed. After playing a card the player does not get to draw a new card. Then the next player plays a card and so forth. When a player can not get a game piece out of home, he is not able to play this round. If all cards are used the round ends.

In the next round each player gets new cards and another round is played. This pattern repeats itself until a player has moved all his game pieces into the garage.

Cards:

  • The game is played with 15 different cards with the following effects:
  • The card play, 1, 11 and 13 can be used to get a game piece from home into the game. 1, 11 and 13 also move the piece 1 or 11 fields forward.
  • The card 4 can also be used to move backwards.
  • The swap card allows you to exchange one of your cards with one of your opponents.
  • The joker card can obtain one of the above card effects.

Rules:

  1. Before a round starts each player gets a set number of cards.
  2. Pieces are moved by the number on the card which is played. If a card with the value 5 is played the player can move a piece by 5 fields. While moving all game pieces can be skipped, but their fields are counted.
  3. There can only be one game piece on a field. When another game piece moves onto an occupied field, the occupying piece gets moved back to home. Furthermore, all game pieces which are overtaken with the card 7 also must be moved back to home.
  4. When a card is played you must move a piece by the provided number. When a player can move into the garage with a 5 but plays a 7, he must complete another round around the board.
  5. Game pieces which have reached the garage can only be moved forward. Game pieces cannot be overtaken in the garage.
  6. If a player has moved all his pieces into the garage, he has won the game.
